MIRL Map

A documentary photo-map

A short walk along the southern edge of UC Santa Barbara, where the campus gives way to bluff, chaparral, and the sea. These photographs return to that seam across a few years and seasons: an entrance monument, a warning sign at a slipping cliff, a storage container left on the sand, the Channel Islands held on the horizon. Read it as a map, or top to bottom as a walk.

Click any photograph on the map for its caption and narrative. Use Search (or press /), the arrow keys (← →) to step through photos, or Wander (W) to jump to a random one.

How to use this map

Map navigation

  • Drag to pan; scroll or pinch to zoom.
  • Click any photo marker to open it in the side drawer.
  • Hover a marker for a brief tooltip and its sightline.

Top bar

  • Map style: switch the base map.
  • 🔍 Search: search captions, filenames, and narratives.
  • ⚄ Wander: open a random photograph (W).
  • ◫ Gallery: read the same material as a photo essay.
  • Aa: text size, high-contrast, reduce-motion options.

Layers panel (☰)

Toggle the optional overlays you have turned on in js/config.js (landmarks, sightlines, area polygons, and so on).

Photo drawer

  • Use ‹ Prev / Next › or the arrow keys.
  • 📋 Cite: export a citation in Chicago, MLA, APA, or BibTeX.
  • The URL updates with ?photo=… — a shareable permalink.
