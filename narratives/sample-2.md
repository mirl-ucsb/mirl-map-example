The sign is doing its job and losing. Coyote brush and coastal sage have grown up around it until the warning is nearly part of the hillside, a yellow rectangle you notice only once you are close enough to ignore it.

This is how the institution speaks to an edge it cannot hold. It cannot stop the cliff from failing, so it posts a notice and hands the risk to whoever reads it. Two pelicans cross the top of the frame, indifferent to the whole arrangement.
