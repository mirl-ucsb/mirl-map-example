Every campus runs on buildings like this one: prefabricated, board-and-batten beige, set down as a temporary fix and kept for decades. It has a small staircase, a single door, and the particular blankness of a structure no one was meant to admire.

In front of it the groundcover is clipped into a flat green rectangle, tidy right up to the point where the maintained campus stops and the bluff begins. The line between the two is no wider than a mower's path.
