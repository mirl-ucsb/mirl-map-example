The black stems and bare ground read as damage, but this is upkeep. Stretches of this mesa are cleared and burned on purpose, to set back the non-native growth and give the native coastal scrub a chance to come back. A single cord, strung at knee height, asks you to keep out while it does.

Behind it a campus tower stands half-dissolved in the marine fog, close and unreachable at once. The restored coast and the built campus share the same few hundred meters, each managed on its own schedule, neither content to be only background to the other.
