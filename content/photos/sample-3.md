A wall of prickly pear has taken the fence for a trellis, its pads heavy with fruit and a few stray bougainvillea blooms that drifted in from somewhere tended. The path narrows here and tips downhill toward the shore.

On a clear enough day the Channel Islands sit low on the horizon. They carried names long before the campus did, and they will outlast the lamppost on the path by a wide margin.

The fence keeps people to the trail and the trail off the failing slope. Even the wildest-looking corner of this coast turns out, on inspection, to be managed.
